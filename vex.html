VEX Robotics

More insight and information into the VEX Robotics platforms used at St Helena Robotics

VEX EDR

VEX EDR is considered to be the premier educational robotics solution and all of our EDR competitors would agree. Students utilise and apply real-world concepts and ideas, adapting them to construct a fully functioning robot. They'll do this whilst developing teamwork and communication skills, often without them even knowing.

The EDR platform is metal-based and requires skills with multiple tools to create a competitive robot. The programming aspect of VEX EDR presents our students with evolving challenges which they work to overcome as they grow more confident with the C++ programming language. The VEX EDR platform develops vital skills that will prove beneficial for every student participating at the club.

VEX Robotics Competition

The VEX Robotics Competition (VRC) tasks students with designing and building a robot to compete against other teams in a game-based engineering challenge. Seasons are presented by the Robotics Education & Competition Foundation, beginning with the game announcement in April and culminating at the VEX Robotics World Championship the following year. Students at St Helena Robotics get to compete at regional tournaments with the chance of qualifying to compete at the national and international level.

This season's VRC game is 'Change Up': The object of the game is to attain a higher score than the opposing Alliance by placing Balls in Goals, and Connecting Rows. Find out more in the video and by using the links below.

VEX IQ

VEX IQ is the intuitive robotics platform that's revolutionising STEM for year 7s and 8s at St Helena Robotics. Thanks to the snap-together, plastic parts, students can construct complete robots within a matter of hours. Participating students begin to grasp countless concepts applicable to real-world situations by engaging with the various aspects of STEM education offered. Furthermore, various programming options are offered to cover those just getting started with a drag-and-drop interface to others who enjoy text-based languages.

VEX IQ Challenge

The VEX IQ Challenge (VIQC) build a robot to play with other teams in a game-based engineering challenge. Seasons are presented by the Robotics Education & Competition Foundation, beginning with the game announcement in April and culminating at the VEX Robotics World Championship the following year. Students at St Helena Robotics get to compete at regional tournaments with the chance of qualifying to compete at the national and international level.
